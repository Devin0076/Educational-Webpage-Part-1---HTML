The Goblin Shark

What is a Goblin Shark?

The Goblin shark is a rare deep-sea shark sometimes called a “living fossil” due to its ancient lineage.

This unusual species was first discovered in the late 19th century off the coast of Japan.

What makes it unique?

  • Distinctive long, flattened snout
  • Protrusible jaws with nail-like teeth
  • Lives in deep ocean waters
  • Pale, almost pinkish skin due to blood vessels showing through

Interesting facts about Goblin Sharks

  1. They can extend their jaws forward rapidly to catch prey.
  2. They typically live at depths greater than 100 meters (330 feet).
  3. The species can grow to over 12 feet long.
  4. They are rarely seen by humans due to their deep-sea habitat.
  5. The oldest fossils of related species date back over 100 million years.
